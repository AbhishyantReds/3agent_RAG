DROMA Electronics General Customer Support Rules:
Service requests can be raised via web chat, email, or helpline.
Business support hours are Monday to Saturday, 9 AM to 7 PM local time.
Refunds are processed only after product inspection and approval.
Approved refunds are credited within 7 to 10 business days.
Customers should back up personal data before submitting devices for repair.

Customer Communication Standards:
All service tickets receive a unique reference ID for tracking.
Customers are notified through SMS or email at each major service stage: ticket creation, inspection, repair start, completion, and dispatch.
If contact attempts fail for 7 consecutive days, the ticket may be marked pending customer response.

Pickup and Drop Policy:
Pickup service availability depends on pin code and product category.
Products should be packed securely before handover; transit damage due to poor packaging may not be covered.
Unclaimed repaired products may attract storage charges after 30 days from completion notice.

Return, Cancellation, and Refund:
Order cancellation is allowed before shipment confirmation.
Returns are accepted only for eligible categories and within the approved return window mentioned on invoice.
Refund mode follows original payment method, subject to payment gateway and banking timelines.
Shipping or installation charges may be non-refundable unless product defect is confirmed at delivery.

Escalation Matrix:
Level 1: Customer support desk for first response and ticket registration.
Level 2: Service coordinator for delayed repair or repeated follow-up issues.
Level 3: Regional support manager for unresolved cases beyond committed SLA.

Privacy and Data Handling:
Devices submitted for service may be reset during diagnostics.
Customers are responsible for removing SIM cards, memory cards, and personal accounts before handover.
DROMA is not liable for unrecoverable personal data loss during repair operations.

Fraud and Misuse Checks:
Tampered invoices, altered serial numbers, or mismatched IMEI records may lead to claim rejection.
Support abuse or repeated false claims can result in account-level support restrictions.
